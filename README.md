## 📝 Description
This repository provides the definitive **DLMC-MAG computational framework (v14.0.2)**, an independent research solution for simulating solar-terrestrial magnetospheric interactions. Moving beyond classical Magnetohydrodynamics (MHD), this framework treats the interplanetary vacuum as a torsional superfluid manifold governed by the **Vortex-T** operator.

The core of the project consists of 30 integrated analytical modules designed to stress-test the terrestrial magnetic shield against Solar Cycle 25 transients. 

### Key features include:
*   **Dynamic Bow Shock Mapping:** High-precision tracking of the magnetopause stand-off distance, validated against the Shue et al. (1997) empirical benchmarks with a 1.00x alignment accuracy.
*   **Substorm Hysteresis Analysis:** Quantification of the Phase-Lag between solar wind pressure jumps and magnetospheric reconfiguration.
*   **Tail Reconnection & Plasmoid Ejection:** Modeling of the topological "tearing instability" and subsequent mass-loss events in the night-side cavity.
*   **Auroral & Ionospheric Coupling:** Mapping of the Field-Aligned Currents (FAC) and the expansion of the auroral oval as a function of vacuum torsion.
*   **Thermodynamic Order:** Evaluation of the Hessian Stability and Bogoliubov Phonon Spectrum to confirm the superfluid integrity of the magnetic cavity.

This work unifies astrophysical shielding with morphogenetic principles (**Project MORPHEUS**), demonstrating that the vacuum is a coherent, "breathing" structure where metric torsion acts as the universal architect of planetary protection.
