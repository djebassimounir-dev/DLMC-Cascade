## 🌌 Overview

**Solar Morveu** provides the definitive **DLMC-MAG computational framework (v14.0.2)**, an independent research solution for simulating solar-terrestrial magnetospheric interactions. 

Moving beyond classical Magnetohydrodynamics (MHD), this framework treats the interplanetary vacuum as a **torsional superfluid manifold** governed by the **Vortex-T** operator. It demonstrates that the vacuum is a coherent, "breathing" structure where metric torsion acts as the universal architect of planetary protection.

This work unifies astrophysical shielding with morphogenetic principles (**Project MORPHEUS**).

## 🚀 Key Features

The core consists of **30 integrated analytical modules** designed to stress-test the terrestrial magnetic shield against **Solar Cycle 25** transients:

*   **Dynamic Bow Shock Mapping:** High-precision tracking of magnetopause stand-off distance, validated against Shue et al. (1997) benchmarks with 1.00x alignment accuracy.
*   **Substorm Hysteresis Analysis:** Quantification of the Phase-Lag between solar wind pressure jumps and magnetospheric reconfiguration.
*   **Tail Reconnection & Plasmoid Ejection:** Modeling of the topological "tearing instability" and subsequent mass-loss events in the night-side cavity.
*   **Auroral & Ionospheric Coupling:** Mapping of Field-Aligned Currents (FAC) and the expansion of the auroral oval as a function of vacuum torsion.
*   **Thermodynamic Order:** Evaluation of the Hessian Stability and Bogoliubov Phonon Spectrum to confirm the superfluid integrity of the magnetic cavity.
